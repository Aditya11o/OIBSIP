# Retail Sales Data - Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Welcome to the **Retail Sales Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** project, developed as part of the Data Analytics Internship at **Oasis Infobyte** (Level 1, Project 1). 

In this project, we perform a detailed visual and statistical investigation of a retail sales transaction history to uncover customer demographic behavior, purchase habits, seasonal trends, and product category performance.

## 📈 Key Insights Summary

1. **Total Revenue**: The store generated a total of **$456,000** in revenue across **1,000 transactions** (2023).
2. **Sales Volatility**: Sales peak in **May ($53,150)** and **October ($46,580)**, but experience a sharp decline in **September ($23,620)**. 
3. **Product Contribution**: 
   - **Electronics** is the leading revenue driver (**$156,905**), thanks to higher unit prices.
   - **Clothing** has the highest volume of transactions (**351**) and total units sold (**894**).
   - **Beauty** is a close third (**$143,515**).
4. **Customer Demographics**:
   - Female customers represent **51.1%** ($232,840) of sales; males represent **48.9%** ($223,160). 
   - Female customers buy significantly more **Beauty** products ($83,860 vs. $59,655).
   - Male customers buy more **Electronics** ($84,650 vs. $72,255).
   - Shoppers aged **18-25** purchase the highest average order value (**$501.01**), while those aged **46-55** generate the highest total order count (**225**).
